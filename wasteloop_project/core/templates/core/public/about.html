About WasteLoop

Our mission, our model, our community.

The Problem

Refugee settlements face severe waste management challenges — limited infrastructure, rapid population growth, and inadequate formal collection systems. Large amounts of waste accumulate, creating health risks and degraded living conditions.

Current interventions are short-term, grant-funded clean-ups that fail to create sustainable impact. Waste is seen as a burden rather than a resource.

The Cycle
  • Unmanaged waste piles up
  • Health and environmental risks grow
  • Short-term grants fund one-off clean-ups
  • Communities remain dependent on external funding

Our Solution

WasteLoop builds a sustainable, community-driven circular economy within refugee settlements — transforming waste into economic opportunity.

We mobilise refugees as waste collectors, connect collected materials to recycling partners, and provide financial incentives that make waste collection a viable income source.

The Circular Model
1
Collect — Refugees gather and sort waste at community points
2
Verify — Waste is weighed and logged at the collection hub
3
Sell — WasteLoop aggregates and sells in bulk to recyclers
4
Pay — Collectors receive fair payment based on weight and type

Why Refugee Settlements?

Untapped Workforce

Large, motivated communities with time and capacity to collect — just needing a fair system to participate in.

Economic Need

Limited formal employment opportunities mean waste collection can become a meaningful, dignified income source.

Environmental Impact

Reducing waste accumulation directly improves health outcomes and living conditions in densely populated settlements.
